# Scoring Rubric

All domain skills use this rubric to score student answers. Apply it consistently.

---

## Scale

| Score | Label | Criteria |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 0 | No understanding | No answer, completely wrong, or restates the question. Cannot identify the relevant concept. |
| 1 | Recognition | Identifies the right concept area but cannot explain how it applies. Uses correct terminology without demonstrating comprehension. |
| 2 | Application | Correctly explains the concept and how it applies to their code. Can describe what would happen if something changed. Minor gaps in edge cases or deeper implications. |
| 3 | Mastery | Explains the concept, its tradeoffs, and alternatives. Can reason about why the current approach was chosen over others. Could teach this to someone else. |

---

## Scoring guidelines

- Score what the student demonstrates, not what you think they know
- A partially correct answer with sound reasoning scores higher than a correct answer with no explanation
- If the student says "I don't know" but then reasons through it and arrives at a correct conclusion, that's a 2 or 3
- If the student gives a correct answer but cannot explain why, that's a 1
- Do not penalize informal language or imprecise terminology if the underlying understanding is clear
- Do not give credit for restating code comments or variable names without demonstrating understanding of the underlying concept

---

## Feedback per question

After scoring each answer, provide:
1. The score (0–3)
2. A one-sentence explanation of why that score was given
3. If scored 0 or 1: a brief pointer toward the concept they should review (not a full explanation — the goal is to guide self-study, not teach in the moment)

---

## Domain summary

After all questions in a domain are complete, produce:
- Total score: sum of all question scores
- Max possible: number of questions × 3
- Percentage: total / max × 100
- Strongest area: which question type(s) they scored highest on
- Gap: which concept(s) they should prioritize for study
